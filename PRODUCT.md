# Product

## Register

product

## Users

STG SMK Penanti is used by school administrators, teachers, subject coordinators, the principal, and students. Most users are completing operational academic tasks during the school day: managing classes, recording marks, scanning OMR sheets, approving results, generating report cards, or checking academic performance.

## Product Purpose

The system centralizes academic marking and reporting for SMK Penanti. It supports user and class management, subject and exam setup, objective and subjective mark entry, OMR grading, coordinator approval, report card generation, and student result viewing. Success means users can complete these workflows with confidence, clear status visibility, and minimal visual friction.

## Brand Personality

Professional, calm, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a reliable school operations system: clear enough for repeated daily use, polished enough for leadership and parent-facing academic outputs, and restrained enough that data and actions remain the focus.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style dashboards, decorative gradients, oversized hero sections inside authenticated pages, overly playful visuals, glassy effects, noisy card grids, and color used as decoration. Do not make data-heavy school workflows feel like a landing page or consumer social app.

## Design Principles

1. Put academic work first: tables, forms, statuses, and actions should be easy to scan and use.
2. Use restrained hierarchy: emphasize primary actions and current state without visual noise.
3. Preserve role clarity: each role should quickly understand where they are and what they can do.
4. Standardize repeated patterns: filters, cards, dialogs, tables, and empty states should share one visual vocabulary.
5. Support dense workflows gracefully: mobile and desktop layouts should remain legible even when pages contain many records or fields.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve visible focus states, keyboard navigation, readable 14px minimum UI text, clear labels, and reduced-motion-friendly transitions. Color should never be the only signal for success, warning, approval, rejection, or error states.
